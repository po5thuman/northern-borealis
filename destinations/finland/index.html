Where Is Finland?

Finland is a country in Northern Europe, bordered by Sweden to the west, Norway to the north and Russia to the east, with the Baltic Sea to the south. Its northernmost region, Lapland, stretches high above the Arctic Circle — and that's exactly where the Northern Lights magic happens.

So where in Finland is Lapland? It's the entire top third of the country: a huge, sparsely populated Arctic wilderness that includes Rovaniemi (the regional capital and home of Santa Claus) and Levi (Finland's leading ski resort). Both sit directly beneath the auroral oval, making them prime aurora borealis territory — browse our Lapland tour comparison here.

Is Finland Scandinavian?

It's a common question. Finland is one of the five Nordic countries, alongside Sweden, Norway, Denmark and Iceland. Strictly speaking, "Scandinavia" refers only to Norway, Sweden and Denmark — so Finland is technically Nordic rather than Scandinavian, even though the terms are often used loosely to include it. Either way, Finland shares the same Arctic beauty, clean design, sauna culture and world-class Northern Lights as its neighbours — see our guides to Norway and Sweden to compare.

What Is Finland Known For?

Beyond the Northern Lights, Finland is famous for a whole host of things that make it an unforgettable place to visit:

Santa Claus and Lapland. Rovaniemi is the official hometown of Santa Claus, and Santa Claus Village sits right on the Arctic Circle — making Finland the ultimate Christmas destination.

Nature and the countryside. Finland is a land of thousands of lakes, endless forests and pristine Arctic wilderness. Its peaceful countryside is perfect for reindeer sightings, husky safaris and, of course, dark-sky aurora hunting.

Saunas and happiness. The sauna is a way of life here, and Finland is regularly ranked the happiest country in the world — a warm, welcoming and remarkably safe place to travel for everyone.

Best Time to Travel to Finland for the Northern Lights

The Finland Northern Lights season runs from late August to early April, with the darkest and clearest skies typically found between September and March. The most magical time of all is the Christmas period in Lapland, when festive traditions, snow-covered landscapes and strong aurora chances all come together. Whenever you go, give yourself a few nights so the weather can cooperate — and book your tours early, as Finland's peak-season and Christmas dates sell out fast.
