Northern Lights Guides

Everything you need to know to plan the perfect aurora borealis trip — from when to go, to where to stay.

Planning a trip to see the Northern Lights can feel overwhelming. Unlike a beach holiday, you can't just show up and expect the magic to happen. You need to align dark skies, solar activity, clear weather, and the right destination inside the Arctic Circle.

We've created this hub of expert guides to cut through the confusion. Whether you are wondering about the science behind the lights, trying to decide which month gives you the best odds, or figuring out exactly how to hunt them once you step off the plane, our step-by-step guides will help you plan your Arctic adventure with confidence.

Put Your Plan into Action

Once you understand when and how to go, the next step is securing your bookings. We independently review and rank the best Northern Lights tours across Europe's top destinations. Choose a country below to explore our comprehensive travel guides and hand-picked tour recommendations.

Norway

Norway is widely considered the heart of Northern Lights tourism. Its coastal climate keeps winter temperatures milder than inland areas, while dramatic fjords provide stunning backdrops.

Top Bases:

  • • Tromsø (The Aurora Capital)
  • • Alta (The City of the Northern Lights)
  • • Svolvær (The Lofoten Islands)
  • • Svalbard (The High Arctic)

Iceland

Iceland sits right on the edge of the Arctic Circle, offering visitors the chance to combine aurora hunting with world-class waterfalls, hot springs, and volcanic landscapes.

Top Bases:

  • • Reykjavík (Perfect for short breaks)
  • • Akureyri (Darker skies in the North)

Sweden

Swedish Lapland is renowned for its incredibly clear weather. Because of the surrounding mountains, it enjoys more cloud-free nights than almost anywhere else in the auroral oval.

Top Bases:

  • • Abisko (Home of the famous "Blue Hole")
  • • Kiruna (Ice hotels and Sami culture)

Finland

Finnish Lapland is the ultimate winter wonderland. It is the perfect destination for families and those looking to combine the Northern Lights with genuine Christmas magic.

Top Bases:

  • • Lapland (Covering Rovaniemi & Levi)

Frequently Asked Questions

🤔 How hard is it to plan a Northern Lights trip?
It requires some planning, as you need to align dark skies, clear weather, and solar activity. However, by choosing a reliable destination in the auroral oval (like Northern Norway, Swedish Lapland, or Iceland) during the winter months, and booking local guides who know the microclimates, you drastically increase your chances.
🚙 Should I book a tour or go hunting on my own?
While you can rent a car and hunt independently, booking a guided Northern Lights tour is highly recommended for first-timers. Guides have real-time satellite weather data, know how to safely navigate icy Arctic roads in the dark, and can often provide thermal clothing and photography assistance.
📅 What is the first step in planning an aurora trip?
The first step is picking the right time of year (typically between late September and late March) and deciding on a destination. Once your flights and hotel are secured, you should book your Northern Lights tours in advance, as top-rated small group tours sell out quickly during the peak winter season.
