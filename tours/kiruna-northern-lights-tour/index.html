Where Is Kiruna, Sweden?

Kiruna is the northernmost city in Sweden, located in Norrbotten County in the heart of Swedish Lapland, well inside the Arctic Circle. Best known worldwide for its iron-ore mine and the nearby ICEHOTEL, Kiruna is also one of the country's most popular bases for Northern Lights holidays — and a short drive from the celebrated Abisko National Park.

Because Kiruna, Norrbotten, Sweden lies directly under the auroral oval, it enjoys long, dark winter nights and frequent aurora activity. Once you leave the city lights behind, the darkness is deep and the skies are wide — perfect conditions for watching the Northern Lights dance overhead.

Why See the Northern Lights in Kiruna?

We've chased the aurora all over Swedish Lapland, and Kiruna is one of our favourite bases. Here's what makes a Northern Lights tour in Kiruna so special:

Comfort meets wilderness. Kiruna is a proper city with hotels, restaurants and the famous ICEHOTEL nearby — yet within minutes you're in pitch-dark Arctic wilderness perfect for aurora viewing. You get the best of both worlds.

It's the gateway to Abisko. Kiruna, Norrbotten sits close to the legendary Abisko National Park and its "blue hole" of clear sky, so many tours can chase the very best conditions in the region.

Rich Sami culture. Kiruna is at the heart of Swedish Sami country, so many tours pair the aurora with authentic food, stories and traditions — turning a light-hunting trip into a deeper cultural experience.

Best Time to See the Northern Lights in Kiruna

The Kiruna Northern Lights season runs from late September to early April. The darkest, clearest skies over Norrbotten are typically found from November through March. Whenever you visit, give yourself a few nights in the Kiruna area so the weather has a chance to cooperate — and book early, as the best tours sell out fast.
