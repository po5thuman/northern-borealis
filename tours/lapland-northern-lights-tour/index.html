Where Is Lapland in Finland?

Lapland is the northernmost region of Finland, stretching well above the Arctic Circle into some of Europe's last great wilderness. When people ask where Lapland is, this is it: a land of endless forests, frozen lakes and long, dark winter nights — the perfect canvas for the Northern Lights.

Finnish Lapland has two standout bases for aurora hunters. Rovaniemi, the region's capital, sits right on the Arctic Circle and is world-famous as the official hometown of Santa Claus. Levi, a couple of hours to the north-west, is Finland's leading ski resort, surrounded by open fells and dark skies. The two are close enough that many travellers experience both on a single Lapland trip.

⚡ A quick, friendly heads-up: Lapland is one of the most popular winter destinations on the planet, and the best small-group tours in Rovaniemi and Levi fill up fast — often weeks ahead, and especially around Christmas and New Year. If you've found dates that work, it's genuinely worth booking sooner rather than later. We've seen too many travellers miss out by waiting.

Santa Claus Village & Lapland at Christmas

No Lapland trip is complete without meeting the big man himself. Rovaniemi is the official hometown of Santa Claus, and just outside the city lies the magical Santa Claus Village, sitting right on the Arctic Circle. So if you've ever wondered where Santa Claus lives — this is the answer. You can meet Santa here year-round, cross the Arctic Circle line, and send a postcard from Santa's own post office.

It's this blend that makes a Lapland Christmas so unforgettable: by day you can visit Santa Claus Village, meet reindeer and huskies, and soak up the festive magic; by night you can chase the Northern Lights across the snow. There's genuinely no more magical place to spend Christmas — and combining a Santa Claus Village visit in Rovaniemi with an evening aurora tour is the perfect Lapland day.

Why See the Northern Lights in Lapland?

We've chased the aurora across the entire Arctic, and Finnish Lapland holds a special place in our hearts. Here's what makes a Northern Lights tour in Lapland so magical:

It sits right under the auroral oval. Both Rovaniemi and Levi lie above the Arctic Circle in the aurora sweet spot, so even moderate solar activity can fill the Lapland sky with colour.

Guaranteed-sighting tours are common. Lapland's top operators are so confident in the region that many offer money-back guarantees and unlimited chasing — something you rarely find elsewhere.

It's Christmas magic and aurora in one. Nowhere else can you meet Santa Claus, ride with reindeer and huskies, and watch the Northern Lights all in a single trip. Lapland is a bucket-list winter destination for the whole family.

Best Time to See the Northern Lights in Lapland

The Lapland Northern Lights season runs from late August to early April, with the darkest and clearest skies typically between September and March. The Christmas period is especially magical in Rovaniemi and Levi, combining festive traditions with strong aurora chances. Whenever you visit, give yourself a few nights so the weather can cooperate — and book early, as Lapland's best tours sell out fast, particularly around the holidays.
